Daloop Midday Pulse – March 19, 2026

March 19, 2026

MIDDAY PULSE

  • U.S. stocks are soft into late morning, with SPY -0.54%, QQQ -0.77%, and DIA -0.68% as traders digest renewed inflation risk from energy and a more cautious rate-cut path.
  • Volatility is elevated with VIX 25.20, while crude is bid: WTI (CL=F) +2.47% near $98.
  • Crypto is risk-off as well: BTC -2.64% and ETH -4.23%.

WHAT'S MOVING NOW

  • TSLA -2.74%: High-beta growth is under pressure as the market reprices the odds of near-term easing.
  • NVDA -1.03%: Semis are slipping with risk appetite fading and positioning crowded after a strong run.
  • VET +15.58%: Energy-linked names are catching bids alongside higher crude.
  • CSIQ -27.48%: Solar is weak amid a broad risk-off tape hitting cyclical and higher-duration names.

SECTOR HEAT MAP

  • Green: Energy (XLE +2.11%) is the standout as oil rallies.
  • Red: Industrials (XLI -1.30%) and Utilities (XLU -0.92%) are lagging, while Financials (XLF -0.46%) and Real Estate (XLRE -0.37%) are also in the red.
  • Flat-ish: Tech (XLK -0.23%) and Health Care (XLV -0.03%) are holding up better than the broad tape.

DEVELOPING STORIES

  • Oil remains the main macro variable: escalating Middle East headlines are keeping a risk premium in energy and feeding inflation concerns.
  • Fed path repricing: commentary and bank forecasts are pushing expected timing for the next cut further out, reinforcing the “higher for longer” narrative.
  • Watch market internals into the close: if energy strength can’t offset broader weakness, afternoon selling could accelerate as hedges get adjusted.

ALERT

  • Energy shock risk is back in focus. If crude extends higher from here, the market’s next move may come from rates and inflation expectations rather than earnings headlines.
