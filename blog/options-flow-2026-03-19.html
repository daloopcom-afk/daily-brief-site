Daloop Options Flow – March 19, 2026

March 19, 2026

OPTIONS FLOW SNAPSHOT – March 19, 2026

Market-Level Read

The tape stayed choppy into the close with SPY and QQQ modestly lower, while IWM outperformed. ^VIX backed off meaningfully on the day, but it’s still elevated versus calm regimes, which matters with quarterly expiration mechanics coming into focus.

From a positioning lens, put demand stayed firm. The latest CBOE gauges (reported for March 18) show Total Put/Call = 1.12 and Equity Put/Call = 0.90. That’s not outright panic, but it’s clearly more defensive than the low-0.6/0.7 style readings you see when call-chasing dominates.

One important calendar overlay: Friday is the quarterly “triple-witching” expiration, and estimates put the notional expiring at $5.7T across index, ETF, and single-stocks. In setups like this, flow can be less about fresh conviction and more about hedging, rolls, and pin-risk management.

NOTABLE FLOW

A number of the most actively traded underlyings today were leveraged/bear ETFs and high-beta vehicles (e.g., TZA, TQQQ, BITO, plus headline single names like NVDA). That mix typically maps to two concurrent behaviors:

  • Portfolio hedging via index/ETF options (especially into quarterly expirations)
  • High-gamma speculation concentrated in liquid, high-volatility tickers

Because several of the widely-followed public “options flow” dashboards are increasingly rendered as interactive tables (and did not reliably expose contract-level prints in a readable format today), the cleanest signal to emphasize is the macro structure of the day’s flow:

  • Elevated put/call ratios (defensive baseline)
  • Concentration in liquid underlyings (SPY/QQQ/NVDA + leveraged ETFs)
  • Expiration-driven hedging/roll dynamics heading into Friday

UNUSUAL ACTIVITY

What stood out was not a single headline sweep, but the pattern of activity:

  • Bear-side and hedging vehicles stayed highly active (TZA, SPDN, and other “risk-off” expressions). When these are among the most traded products, it often indicates traders are paying up for convexity rather than trying to pick exact bottoms.
  • Volatility came in, but hedging didn’t disappear. A falling VIX with still-elevated put/call ratios is often consistent with traders owning puts already and taking some vol off, while maintaining protection into a known event (quarterly expiration).

Net: expect the highest “signal-to-noise” contracts to cluster around very near-dated expirations (0DTE/1DTE/weekly) where volume can overwhelm prior OI, creating sharp dealer-hedging feedback loops.

EARNINGS-RELATED POSITIONING

While this note is primarily about flow, it’s worth flagging near-term catalysts that can create asymmetric positioning demand. On the calendar, a few names drawing attention around the window include FedEx (FDX) (upcoming earnings listed for March 19) and New Fortress Energy (NFE) (upcoming earnings listed for March 23). In these situations, traders often express views via short-dated straddles/strangles (vol bets) rather than pure direction, especially if implied volatility is already bid.

Bottom Line

Today’s options tone reads defensive-to-neutral: put demand remains elevated, and the most active underlyings skew toward hedging and high-beta liquidity rather than broad-based call-chasing. With quarterly expiration on deck, treat tape-driven “whale” narratives with extra caution; a meaningful portion of large prints into triple-witching can be mechanical (rolls, hedges, or close-outs) rather than new risk.
