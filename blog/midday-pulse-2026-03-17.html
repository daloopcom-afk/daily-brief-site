Daloop Midday Pulse – March 17, 2026

March 17, 2026

MIDDAY PULSE

  • Stocks are modestly higher: SPY 671.09 (+0.31%), QQQ 603.37 (+0.50%), DIA 471.34 (+0.22%), IWM 249.38 (+0.19%).
  • Risk is mixed under the surface: crypto is softer (BTC 74,144, -0.99%; ETH 2,328, -1.05%), while equities grind higher into the Fed.
  • Tone check: market sentiment reads UNCERTAIN as traders balance “Fed day” positioning with ongoing energy/geopolitical headlines.

WHAT'S MOVING NOW

  • ISPR (+27%) and BRAI (+26%) are pacing the tape among smaller caps, with momentum-driven flows dominating the leaderboard.
  • CTRN (+18%) and LMND (+17%) are notable upside movers, signaling selective risk-taking even with headline risk elevated.
  • On the downside, AP (-28%), MVST (-27%), and CMTL (-27%) are among the sharpest decliners, reflecting idiosyncratic selloffs rather than a broad de-risking.
  • NVDA is among the most active names and slightly red on the day; INTC is notably lower, a reminder that the semiconductor complex remains choppy despite the broader index bid.

SECTOR HEAT MAP

  • Green leaders: Energy (XLE +1.55%) is leading as crude stays a key macro driver; Real Estate (XLRE +1.02%) is also firm.
  • Steady/positive: Financials (XLF +0.62%), Tech (XLK +0.44%), and Utilities (XLU +0.38%) are holding up.
  • Lagging: Health Care (XLV -0.70%) is the main red pocket in today’s sector read.

DEVELOPING STORIES

  • Fed meeting (day 1): with the decision due tomorrow, markets are focused less on the expected “no change” and more on any hints about the path for cuts and how policymakers are thinking about energy-driven inflation.
  • Oil/geopolitics remain the swing factor: traders continue to monitor Middle East developments and any risks to shipping/energy flows; that’s keeping positioning cautious and volatility prone to sudden spikes.
  • Into-the-close watchlist: if equities stay pinned higher while crypto stays weak, watch for late-day rotation (quality/defensives vs. high beta) and any acceleration in energy-led breadth.

ALERT

  • Cross-asset divergence: equities are green while BTC/ETH are down ~1%—if that gap widens, it can be an early tell for risk appetite fading into the afternoon.
