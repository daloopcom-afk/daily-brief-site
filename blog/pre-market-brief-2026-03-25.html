Daloop Pre-Market Brief – March 25, 2026

March 25, 2026

MARKET OVERVIEW

  • US equity futures are slightly lower ahead of the open after Tuesday’s broad-based rally; SPY -0.34%, QQQ -0.68%, DIA -0.17%, while small caps are firmer (IWM +0.54%).
  • Rate backdrop is modestly tighter versus yesterday’s close: 5Y 4.03% (+8 bps), 10Y 4.39% (+6 bps), 30Y 4.94% (+3 bps).
  • Crypto is mixed/quiet: BTC ~71.3k (-0.01%), ETH ~2.18k (+0.86%).
  • Commodities: oil is higher pre-market (energy complex bid) while gold is flat (GLD roughly unchanged).
  • Mega-cap tape is soft in pre-market: GOOGL -3.89%, MSFT -2.73%, META -1.90%, AMZN -1.43%, NVDA -0.27%.
  • Energy is the bright spot pre-market: XOM +2.59%, CVX +0.77%.
  • Risk tone: mixed to slightly risk-off (mega-cap weakness + higher yields), with an energy bid keeping cyclicals supported.

TODAY'S MACRO & EVENTS

  • 8:30am ET – Import/Export Prices (Feb): A direct read on pipeline inflation and how much the energy move is feeding into goods prices.
  • 10:00am ET – MBA Mortgage Applications / Rates: A weekly pulse on housing demand sensitivity to rates.
  • 8:30am ET – Current Account: Helps frame external financing needs and USD balance-of-payments dynamics.
  • 4:10pm ET – Fed Gov. Miran (Digital Asset Summit): Any incremental signal on financial conditions, balance sheet themes, or risk sentiment can move rates and the dollar late-day.

KEY STOCK / CRYPTO MOVERS

  • GOOGL -3.89% (pre): Weakness is weighing on the broader mega-cap complex into the open.
  • MSFT -2.73% (pre): Following the same tape as large-cap tech; watch whether the selling stays concentrated in megas vs broadens to semis/software.
  • XOM +2.59% (pre): Energy bid continues as crude rebounds; energy leadership is becoming the key offset to tech softness.
  • ETH +0.86% (24h): Crypto is holding up better than equities; watch if it decouples further if rates stay firm.
  • PAYX (earnings before open): Payroll/SMB demand read-through; listen for commentary on client retention, hiring, and wage pressure.

THEMES & WATCHLIST IDEAS

  • Higher-for-longer rates / duration pressure: MSFT, META, AMZN, QQQ. Watch whether yields keep grinding higher into 8:30am and whether growth underperforms again.
  • Energy rebound / geopolitical premium: XOM, CVX, USO. Watch for follow-through if crude extends yesterday’s rebound.
  • Small-cap relative strength: IWM, regional banks, cyclicals. If megas stay heavy but small caps hold, it’s a “rotation tape,” not a broad risk-off.
  • China consumer / cross-border e-commerce earnings read-through: PDD (earnings). Watch for demand/margin commentary that hits broader China ADR sentiment.

WHAT CHANGED SINCE YESTERDAY

  • The pre-market leadership flipped toward energy while mega-cap tech is weaker, creating a more rotational (less index-level) tape.
  • Treasury yields moved higher (5Y/10Y/30Y all up), tightening financial conditions at the margin.
  • The market mood is more uncertain heading into today’s data and event slate.

ONE-PARAGRAPH TL;DR

Futures are modestly lower with a rotational feel: mega-cap tech is soft while energy is bid as crude rebounds. Yields are higher across the curve, keeping duration-sensitive growth under pressure. Today’s focus is on the 8:30am ET inflation-related prints (import/export prices), mortgage applications, and a late-day Fed event that could nudge rates sentiment. Crypto is steady-to-firmer, and small caps are showing relative resilience.
