Daloop Closing Bell – March 18, 2026

March 18, 2026

CLOSING BELL – March 18, 2026

FINAL SCORES

  • SPY fell 1.37% to 661.60 (range 661.27–669.72) as sellers controlled the tape into the close.
  • QQQ dropped 1.39% to 594.90 (range 594.56–603.15) with growth unable to reclaim the morning highs.
  • DIA slid 1.65% to 463.13 (range 462.76–472.36) as cyclicals weighed on the Dow.
  • IWM lost 1.61% to 246.02 (range 245.88–249.23) as small caps stayed under pressure.
  • Volatility jumped: the VIX rose to 24.65 (+10.19%), a reminder that hedging demand is back.

SESSION NARRATIVE

Stocks sold off Wednesday as a one-two punch of hotter inflation data and a firmer Fed message tightened financial conditions. February Producer Price Index surprised to the upside, reinforcing the market’s fear that inflation isn’t cooling fast enough for the Fed to begin easing soon.

The Fed held rates steady at 3.5%–3.75%, and Chair Powell’s tone did not help risk appetite. Traders parsed the updated projections for confirmation that the bar for cuts remains high, and Powell’s comments about the need for “more progress” on inflation accelerated the afternoon slide.

Energy was the other macro overhang. Crude spiked again on escalating Middle East conflict headlines, keeping the inflation narrative in play and pressuring longer-term rates. The 10-year yield ended higher on the day, consistent with the market’s “higher for longer” repricing.

TOP MOVERS AT THE CLOSE

  • NFE sank about 20% after another rough session for the name, extending a sharp drawdown.
  • DVLT surged nearly 20% as speculative small-cap momentum remained active despite broader risk-off tone.
  • TLYS gained more than 23%, outperforming on company-specific strength.
  • GSIT fell about 30%, one of the day’s steepest decliners.
  • SWMR jumped more than 77% in a high-volatility move typical of thin-float names.

SECTOR PERFORMANCE

  • Defensives did not provide much shelter: XLV led the downside (-1.67%) while XLF (-1.19%) and XLK (-1.13%) also struggled.
  • Industrials were relatively resilient (XLI -0.79%).
  • Energy was steadier than the broader tape (XLE -0.14%) even as crude climbed.

AFTER-HOURS WATCH

Crypto mirrored the risk-off mood: Bitcoin fell about 4.1% and Ethereum dropped about 6.2%. With volatility rising across assets, the market is sensitive to any incremental hawkishness and to any new developments in energy markets.

Earnings season remains a near-term catalyst, with investors closely watching results from bellwethers tied to AI and consumer demand for clues on whether margin pressure from higher input costs is starting to show up in guidance.

TOMORROW’S SETUP

  • Key watch items: follow-through in rates (especially if yields continue grinding higher), crude’s next move, and whether volatility cools or stays elevated.
  • If the dollar stays firm and oil remains bid, expect the market to keep testing downside levels until there’s clearer evidence inflation is bending lower.
