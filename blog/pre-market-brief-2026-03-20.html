Daloop Pre-Market Brief – March 20, 2026

March 20, 2026

MARKET OVERVIEW

  • US index ETFs are slightly lower pre-market: SPY -0.25%, QQQ -0.32%, DIA -0.42%, while small caps are firmer (IWM +0.65%).
  • Risk tone is mixed: macro-sensitive cyclicals are not collapsing, but mega-cap tech is dragging the tape.
  • Rates are nudging higher in the belly: 5Y ~3.92%, 10Y ~4.28%, while the long end is slightly lower (30Y ~4.85%).
  • Commodities are the weak spot: USO -3.54% and GLD -4.12% point to a broad de-risking / positioning reset in hard assets.
  • Crypto is holding up better than equities: BTC +0.92% and ETH ~flat.
  • Single-name pressure is concentrated in high-beta / AI-adjacent leadership (e.g., TSLA, MU, META, NVDA).

TODAY'S MACRO & EVENTS

  • 10:00am ET: Employer Costs for Employee Compensation (Dec 2025) — wage/benefits inflation signal that can feed into the rates path and the market’s tolerance for higher-for-longer.

KEY STOCK / CRYPTO MOVERS

  • MU -3.78% (pre-market): Pullback after a strong run despite a headline beat; the move reads like positioning/profit-taking and sensitivity to incremental capex / supply commentary.
  • TSLA -3.18% (pre-market): High-beta downside continues; watch for any headlines on deliveries, pricing, or regulatory/autonomy developments as the stock remains a macro + sentiment barometer.
  • META -1.46% (pre-market): Large-cap weakness adds pressure to QQQ; flows matter here given how crowded mega-cap exposure remains.
  • GLD -4.12% (pre-market): Sharp move lower implies fast deleveraging in metals/miners complex; watch correlated pressure in gold equities.
  • BTC +0.92% (spot): Relative strength versus equities; watch if it stays bid even as rate-sensitive assets wobble.

THEMES & WATCHLIST IDEAS

  • AI compute & semis: NVDA, MU, MSFT, AMZN — watch whether “good news gets sold” continues; MU/NVDA price action can spill into the broader complex.
  • Energy / geopolitics premium: XOM, CVX, USO — oil is weak pre-market; watch if headlines reprice supply risk back in quickly.
  • Mega-cap tech beta: AAPL, GOOGL, META, MSFT — if these keep leaking, index-level rebounds will be harder to sustain.
  • Small-cap resilience: IWM (and high-volume small-cap ETFs) — if IWM keeps outperforming while QQQ fades, it’s a notable rotation signal.

WHAT CHANGED SINCE YESTERDAY

  • Commodities are seeing outsized downside relative to equity indices (oil and gold are leading the weakness).
  • Small caps are showing relative strength versus large-cap tech pre-market.
  • Treasuries are mixed with a modest uptick in intermediate yields.
  • AI-linked leaders are seeing morning profit-taking / de-risking pressure.

ONE-PARAGRAPH TL;DR

Pre-market is choppy and slightly risk-off at the index level, with SPY/QQQ/DIA modestly lower but IWM holding up. The rates backdrop is mixed, with intermediate yields edging higher, while commodities are the standout weak spot (oil and gold are both down sharply). Single-name pressure is concentrated in high-beta leadership, including MU, TSLA, and META, while crypto is relatively resilient. The key macro item today is the 10:00am ET employment cost release, which will be watched as a wage-inflation input into the rates narrative.
