OPTIONS FLOW SNAPSHOT – March 17, 2026

Market-Level Read

Index and mega-cap flow skewed decisively toward calls today, with the most concentrated activity showing up in near-term expirations (0–3 DTE) and in deep-in-the-money contracts that behave more like leveraged stock exposure than pure volatility bets. That pattern typically reads as “directional positioning / delta exposure” rather than tail hedging.

A key caveat: the snapshot data below reflects the top listed contracts returned by the feed (not the entire chain), so treat this as a directional pulse check on where the loudest prints are clustering.

UNUSUAL ACTIVITY

These stand out because volume is far larger than open interest (often a proxy for new positioning rather than churn):

  • AAPL — 227.5 Call, Exp 3/16: volume ~216 vs OI ~10
  • QQQ — 515 Call, Exp 3/17: volume ~169 with minimal reported OI
  • TQQQ — 32 Call, Exp 3/20: volume ~126 with minimal reported OI
  • AAPL — 200 Call, Exp 3/16: volume ~78 vs OI ~1

Why it matters: when volume dwarfs OI in very short-dated contracts, the flow often reflects traders taking a strong view on an imminent catalyst window (macro tape, positioning into the close, or a rapid response to intraday price action), rather than slow-building longer-term conviction.

EARNINGS-RELATED POSITIONING

The next major earnings cluster for several high-flow names is in late April. As of today’s schedule, TSLA is slated for 4/21, INTC for 4/23, and META for 4/29. Given that most of today’s highest-intensity prints are expiring this week, the positioning looks more like near-term tape trading than earnings hedging.

If flow begins migrating out to late-April expirations over the next couple of weeks, that would be a clearer signal that traders are shifting from “daily delta” to “event vol” positioning.
