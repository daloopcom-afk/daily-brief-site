Daloop Pre-Market Brief – March 23, 2026

March 23, 2026

MARKET OVERVIEW

  • US equity tone is risk-off pre-market: SPY -1.70%, QQQ -1.85%, DIA -1.12%, IWM -2.18%.
  • Big Tech is tracking lower with semis leading downside: NVDA -3.15%, TSLA -3.24%, GOOGL -2.00%, META -2.15%, MSFT -1.84%, AMZN -1.62%.
  • Macro backdrop is tighter-rate sensitive: Treasury yields are higher with 5Y 4.01%, 10Y 4.39%, 30Y 4.96%.
  • Energy is a relative bright spot: USO +3.47%, with majors modestly firmer (XOM +1.01%, CVX +0.17%).
  • Gold is offered: GLD -3.06%, consistent with broad de-risking and higher real-rate pressure.
  • Crypto is mixed: BTC +0.72% while ETH -0.49%.
  • Risk tone check: headline-driven and defensive, with markets leaning toward higher-volatility outcomes into the week.

TODAY'S MACRO & EVENTS

  • 10:00 AM ET — Construction Spending (Residential), Jan (prior: +1.5%): Housing-sensitive series that can shift the growth narrative and rate expectations at the margin.
  • 10:00 AM ET — Construction Spending (Total), Jan (prior: +0.3%): Read-through to capex/investment and a small input into GDP tracking.

KEY STOCK / CRYPTO MOVERS

  • SMCI -33%: Selling pressure continues after headlines around alleged illegal export of AI-chip servers to China; investors are repricing regulatory and customer-risk.
  • MU -4.81%: Post-earnings digestion continues after a sharp run-up; market appears focused on whether peak memory pricing and HBM momentum can stay intact.
  • NVDA -3.15%: Semis remain a volatility amplifier in risk-off tapes; also tied to broader “AI trade” de-leveraging.
  • USO +3.47%: Oil exposure is bid as geopolitical supply risk remains elevated.
  • BTCUSD +0.72%: Holding up better than high-beta tech in the tape, but still trading headline-to-headline.

THEMES & WATCHLIST IDEAS

  • Geopolitics / Energy shock (XOM, CVX, USO): Watch whether crude strength persists into US hours; sustained oil >$100 keeps inflation and Fed optionality front-and-center.
  • AI hardware + export controls (NVDA, SMCI, DELL): Watch for secondary read-throughs (customer re-ordering, margin risk) and any regulatory follow-ups.
  • Rate-sensitive growth reset (QQQ, TSLA, AMZN): Higher yields + risk-off headlines is the mix that typically pressures duration.
  • Defensive hedges vs. “all-assets down” (GLD, SPY): Gold weakness alongside equities suggests liquidity-driven selling; watch for stabilization if rates cool.

WHAT CHANGED SINCE YESTERDAY

  • Oil-linked risk premia remains the dominant cross-asset driver, keeping a defensive bias into Monday.
  • Semis/AI complex is again a main source of index-level beta (downside leadership in NVDA/MU and SMCI-specific pressure).
  • Yields are higher across the curve, tightening financial conditions as the week starts.

ONE-PARAGRAPH TL;DR

Pre-market is risk-off with broad weakness across major US ETFs and high-beta tech, while energy is the main relative winner. Rates are higher across the curve, adding pressure to duration-sensitive growth as traders price a more inflationary, headline-driven regime. The key watch is whether elevated oil stays sticky and feeds into rates and equity risk premia, while semis remain the likely volatility lever for index direction at the open.
